To make use of all monitoring features provided by Pumpic for Android, rooting is required. The process is fast and easy. It takes some simple steps to complete.

NOTE: Rooting may void warranty. Done wrong, rooting may brick your device. To avoid this, we suggest you use trusted service OneClickRoot, which is supported by the majority of Android devices. To check compatibility of your device follow this link.

OneClickRoot fee is $39.95 per device for remote rooting by certified Android technicians, 30-days 24/7 support via live chat and by phone, rooting with alternative software.

OneClickRoot is not compatible with all Android devices. If your particular device turned out to be incompatible, feel free to use any alternative rooting service. Here are some of them to consider:

  • Towelroot - http://www.towelroot.com.
  • Kingo Root - http://www.kingoapp.com/index.htm.
  • KingRoot - https://kingroot.net/.

NOTE: Rooting is provided by the third-party software, and Pumpic cannot be responsible for it.

First, you need to prepare your device. Please, complete the following steps:

Set up an Android Device

Important: The target device must have at least 60% of battery life left for rooting purposes.

Please back up your personal information before proceeding with rooting to prevent any data loss.

Activate Developer Options

  1. Go to “Settings” menu.

  2. Proceed to “About phone” section.

  3. Tap on “Build number” 5 times.

  4. You will see a notification “You are now a developer!”
    It means that “Developer options” are activated.

Enable USB Debugging

  1. Choose “Developer options” from the “Settings” menu.

     

  2. Enable “USB debugging”.

  3. A pop-up “Allow USB debugging?” will appear.
    Tap “OK”.

  4. “USB debugging” is enabled.

Enable Unknown Sources

  1. Choose “Security” from the “Settings” menu.

     

  2. Enable “Unknown sources”.

  3. A pop-up will appear.
    Tap “OK”.

  4. “Unknown sources” option is activated.

Use OneClickRoot Software

Important: The rooting procedure requires additional software installation onto your PC.

  1. Download the software on your PC and follow the further instructions: http://pumpic.oneclickroot.revenuewire.net/oneclickroot/download/.
  2. Proceed with the installation. If it is successful, you will see a Superuser icon on a desktop.

A pop-up requesting for the Superuser rights will appear shortly. Tap "Grant"  to provide. If it doesn't appear for a few minutes, run Pumpic. The app will ask for the Superuser rights. Tap "Grant"  to provide.

If you face difficulties with the aforementioned services, or they will not support the target device you are going to monitor, you will have to spend some more time finding out how to root it on Android related forums*. The best place to start with is XDA Developers Forum – http://forum.xda-developers.com. Look for a thread on your particular device to find a method that has worked for other people. If there is no reference on this forum, chances are there are no instructions on rooting your specific device yet at all. Note that some rooting instructions might cause complete device breakdown. So please consider that you perform rooting at your own peril. The other way is to consult with OneClickRoot service since they can root your device even if it's not supported by OneClickRoot software.

Pumpic is worth spending some more time searching. You will not regret it!

*For your convenience, here are some links to instructions on rooting the most popular device models (Nexus 5, Nexus 5x, Nexus 6, Nexus 6P, Nexus 7, Galaxy S5, Galaxy Note 4).

Are you done? Please wait for 20 minutes until Pumpic starts collecting the data.
