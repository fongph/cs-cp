1. What is two-factor authentication for the Apple ID?

    Two-factor authentication is an extra layer of security for your Apple ID designed to ensure that you're the only person who can access your account, even if someone knows your password. With two-factor authentication, your account can only be accessed on devices you trust, like your iPhone, iPad, or Mac. When you want to sign in to the account on a new device for the first time, you'll need to provide two pieces of information - your password and the six-digit verification code that's automatically displayed on your trusted devices. By entering the code, you're verifying that you trust the new device. You can find more information here.

    Two-factor authentication is available to iCloud users with at least one device using iOS 9 or later. Users who don't have such devices, or canâ€™t update their devices, or are otherwise ineligible for two-factor authentication, may protect their Apple ID with two-step verification.

  2. How to set up Pumpic with two-factor authentication?

    If two-factor authentication is enabled on the target Apple ID, you will need to generate and enter the Verification Code after you enter your target Apple ID and password.

    To generate Verification Code, take the target device:

    1. Go to Settings > iCloud, tap on Apple ID and enter iCloud password (if requested).

    2. On the next screen, tap on Password & Security and scroll to find Get Verification Code section.

    3. Tap Get Verification Code. You will be instantly shown a six-digit code.

    4. Type the Verification Code in the requested form in your Control Panel.

    5. Click Update button.

  3. What if I enabled two-factor authentication on the device, which is already monitored?

    Two-factor authentication error will be displayed in your Control Panel. Please, log in to your personal account at cp.pumpic.com, go to Device Settings and click on "validate iCloud account in our system". Then enter your target Apple ID and password. If two-factor authentication is enabled on the target Apple ID, you will need to generate and enter the Verification Code.

    To generate Verification Code, you must:

    1. Go to Settings > iCloud, tap on Apple ID in your iCloud credentials and enter your iCloud password (if requested).

    2. On the next screen, tap on Password & Security and scroll to find Get Verification Code section.

    3. Tap Get Verification Code. You will be instantly shown a six-digit code.

    4. Type the Verification Code in the requested form in your Control Panel.

    5. Click on Update.

  4. What if I don't have access to the target device?

    If you don't have access to the target device, you can generate the Verification Code on any trusted device connected to the same Apple ID or in your Apple ID page. In order to get the Verification code via your Apple ID page, please, log in to appleid.apple.com, go to Security section and click Generate Password.

  5. How to disable two-factor authentication?

    As an alternative solution, you may disable two-factor authentication on the target Apple ID. To turn it off, sign in to the target Apple ID account page and click Edit in the Security section. Then click Turn Off Two-Factor Authentication. After you create new security questions and verify your date of birth, two-factor authentication will be turned off.
